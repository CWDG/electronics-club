Welcome the Electronics Club at The Ohio State University! Our organization is dedicated to fostering interest in the field of electronics in addition to educating students. We aim to get students excited about building their own projects and attending our various student-led workshops! The club has its own workspace equipped with oscilloscopes, signal generators as well as rudimentary tools, and components accessible to members. We also allot members storage space for their personal projects. thattheir latest project Open Lab Instructors hold open lab hours in which students can come in and work on their projects, ask questions or hangout! The club meets every other week, to candidly discuss numerous technologies and standards. Prior meetings have shed light on Serial Protocols, PWM, Arduinos, and more. We urge you to come stop by, our meetings provide excellent learning oppertunities for the novice as well the expert.

Membership

Electronics Club is easy to join, and it's a blast once you do! You can subscribe to our mailing list, come to meetings, pay membership dues ($10/semester, includes use of the electronics lab), and that's it!

Classes

Soon, the Electronics Club will actually be offering classes related to electronics hobbies. We intend to have a soldering class at some point, as well as an introduction to arduino class.

Location

It can be difficult finding the room for the first time. To find the room, enter through the main doors on Neil Avenue. Go past the double doors on your right and take the stairs down to the basement. Turn right around the stairs so that you are going towards a single door down a hallway. Go through the door and continue going straight through the large doors at the end of the hallway. You are now in the Power Lab. Continue straight up the stairs immediately in front of you. Turn right and we are the second door from your left (not the big red door), we're in room number 078.
