Welcome to the Electronics Club at The Ohio State University. Our organization is committed to fostering interest in the field of electronics. The club has its own workspace equipped with oscilloscopes, signal generators, rudimentary tools, and components which are accessible to members. We also allot storage space to members for their personal projects and offer open lab hours for students to work on their projects, ask questions or just hangout! The club meets every other week to candidly discuss numerous technologies and standards. In prior meetings we have discussed topics such as Serial Protocols, PWM, and Arduinos. We urge you to stop by, as our meetings provide excellent learning opportunities for all experience levels.

Membership

Electronics Club is easy to join, not to mention free. Subscribe to our mailing list and attend our next meeting. Active members can rent tools, take components for projects, and submit to the resume database. Active membership status is a semester-long condition found by attending at least two workshops per semester or volunteering for E-Council events in the club's name. Members who are interested are welcome to take on a leadership role by leading workshops or talks and are allowed 24/7 lab room access.

Location

Our club room is located in the basement of Caldwell Laboratory, room 078.
To find the room, enter through the main entrance of Caldwell. Proceed through the hallway until you reach the first staircaise on your right. Walk down the stairs and enter through the large doors that have a sign for the Electronics Club and CHPPE's High Voltage lab. Continue straight through this door, up the small set of stairs and to the right. Our room is the second door from the left, (right of the big red door) room 078 .
